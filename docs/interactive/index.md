# Interactive Practice

Use the tool below to practice dimensional analysis problems. 

- Drag number and unit cards into the factor chain
- Build your conversion 
- Click **cancellation** button to see which units cancel
- Click **calculate** button to get result
- Check your answer.



### Quick Guide


??? info "1. What info do I need?"
    Identify the **given value** and the **target unit**. You may need additional information like conversion rates. 

   | Problem type | Given | Target |
    |---|---|---|
    | Unit conversion | 500 mg | g |
    | Oral medication | Order 750 mg, supply 250 mg/tablet | tablets |
    | Weight-based | 5 mg/kg, patient 60 kg | mg |
    | IV therapy | 1000 mL over 8 hr | mL/hr |


??? info "2. Writing factors"
    Write each piece of information as a fraction. Every number needs a unit — top or bottom. Flip any factor if needed.


    - A single value: **500 mg** becomes `500 mg / 1`
    - A rate: **250 mg/tablet** stays as `250 mg / 1 tablet`
    - A compound rate: **5 mg/kg/min** becomes `5 mg / 1 kg · min`

    Flip any factor if needed — multiplying by `1 g / 1000 mg` is the same as `1000 mg / 1 g`, just upside down.


For example: $\dfrac{250 \text{ mg}}{1 \text{ tablet}}$ or flipped: $\dfrac{1 \text{ tablet}}{250 \text{ mg}}$



??? info "3. Checking unit cancellation"
    A unit cancels when it appears in both a numerator and a denominator. Cross off matching pairs — what remains should be your target unit.
    
    $$\dfrac{500 \text{ mg}}{1} \times \dfrac{1 \text{ g}}{1000 \text{ mg}} = \dfrac{500}{1000} \text{ g} = 0.5 \text{ g}$$

??? info "4. Do the math!"
    Multiply all numerator values together, then divide by all denominator values.

    $$\text{Answer} = \frac{n_1 \times n_2 \times \cdots}{d_1 \times d_2 \times \cdots}$$

    Don't forget — every factor has a number, even if it's 1. Account for all of them.
