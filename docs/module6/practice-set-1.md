## Section 1 — Unit Conversions

!!! example "Problem 1"
    Convert 2.5 g to mg.

??? success "Answer"
    \[2.5 \cancel{\text{ g}} \times \frac{1000 \text{ mg}}{1 \cancel{\text{ g}}} = 2500 \text{ mg}\]

!!! example "Problem 2"
    Convert 750 mcg to mg.

??? success "Answer"
    \[750 \cancel{\text{ mcg}} \times \frac{1 \text{ mg}}{1000 \cancel{\text{ mcg}}} = 0.75 \text{ mg}\]

!!! example "Problem 3"
    A patient weighs 143 lb. Convert to kg.

??? success "Answer"
    \[143 \cancel{\text{ lb}} \times \frac{1 \text{ kg}}{2.2 \cancel{\text{ lb}}} = 65 \text{ kg}\]

## Section 2 — Oral Tablets

!!! example "Problem 4"
    Order: metoprolol 100 mg orally
    Stock: 50 mg per tablet
    How many tablets?

??? success "Answer"
    \[\frac{100 \cancel{\text{ mg}}}{1} \times \frac{1 \text{ tablet}}{50 \cancel{\text{ mg}}} = 2 \text{ tablets}\]

!!! example "Problem 5"

    Stock: 5 mg per tablet

??? success "Answer"
    \[\frac{7.5 \cancel{\text{ mg}}}{1} \times \frac{1 \text{ tablet}}{5 \cancel{\text{ mg}}} = 1.5 \text{ tablets}\]
    
    Verify tablet is scored before splitting.

## Section 4 — Weight-Based Dosing (Adult)

!!! example "Problem 10"
    Order: gentamicin 5 mg/kg IV
    Patient weight: 80 kg
    Stock: 40 mg/mL
    How many mL?

??? success "Answer"
    \[\frac{80 \cancel{\text{ kg}}}{1} \times \frac{5 \cancel{\text{ mg}}}{1 \cancel{\text{ kg}}} \times \frac{1 \text{ mL}}{40 \cancel{\text{ mg}}} = 10 \text{ mL}\]

!!! example "Problem 11"
    Order: vancomycin 15 mg/kg IV
    Patient weight: 176 lb
    Stock: 500 mg/10 mL
    How many mL?

??? success "Answer"
    Convert weight:
    \[176 \cancel{\text{ lb}} \times \frac{1 \text{ kg}}{2.2 \cancel{\text{ lb}}} = 80 \text{ kg}\]
    
    Full chain:
    \[\frac{80 \cancel{\text{ kg}}}{1} \times \frac{15 \cancel{\text{ mg}}}{1 \cancel{\text{ kg}}} \times \frac{10 \text{ mL}}{500 \cancel{\text{ mg}}} = 24 \text{ mL}\]

!!! example "Problem 12"
    Order: ibuprofen 10 mg/kg orally, max 600 mg
    Patient weight: 85 kg
    Stock: 200 mg/5 mL
    How many mL?

??? success "Answer"
    Calculated dose:
    \[85 \cancel{\text{ kg}} \times \frac{10 \text{ mg}}{1 \cancel{\text{ kg}}} = 850 \text{ mg}\]
    
    850 mg exceeds max of 600 mg — **use 600 mg**
    
    Volume:
    \[\frac{600 \cancel{\text{ mg}}}{1} \times \frac{5 \text{ mL}}{200 \cancel{\text{ mg}}} = 15 \text{ mL}\]

!!! example "Problem 13"
    Order: morphine 0.1 mg/kg IV PRN
    Patient weight: 154 lb
    Stock: 10 mg/mL
    How many mL?

??? success "Answer"
    Convert weight:
    \[154 \cancel{\text{ lb}} \times \frac{1 \text{ kg}}{2.2 \cancel{\text{ lb}}} = 70 \text{ kg}\]
    
    Full chain:
    \[\frac{70 \cancel{\text{ kg}}}{1} \times \frac{0.1 \cancel{\text{ mg}}}{1 \cancel{\text{ kg}}} \times \frac{1 \text{ mL}}{10 \cancel{\text{ mg}}} = 0.7 \text{ mL}\]

!!! example "Problem 14"
    Order: amoxicillin 25 mg/kg orally

??? success "Answer"

??? success "Answer"

    Order: penicillin V 50 mg/kg/day orally
    divided every 6 hours
    Patient: child weighing 20 kg
    Stock: 125 mg/5 mL

??? success "Answer"

    \[1000 \div 4 = 250 \text{ mg per dose}\]
    
    Volume:

## Section 6 — IV Flow Rates

!!! example "Problem 17"
    Order: 1000 mL 0.9% NaCl over 8 hours
    What is the flow rate in mL/hr?

??? success "Answer"
    \[\frac{1000 \text{ mL}}{8 \text{ hr}} = 125 \text{ mL/hr}\]

!!! example "Problem 18"
    Order: 250 mL over 45 minutes
    What is the flow rate in mL/hr?

??? success "Answer"
    Convert time:
    \[45 \cancel{\text{ min}} \times \frac{1 \text{ hr}}{60 \cancel{\text{ min}}} = 0.75 \text{ hr}\]
    
    Rate:
    \[\frac{250 \text{ mL}}{0.75 \text{ hr}} = 333.3 \text{ mL/hr}\]
    
    Round to: **333 mL/hr**

!!! example "Problem 19"
    A 1000 mL bag is running at 150 mL/hr.
    After 3 hours how much fluid remains
    and how much longer will it run?

??? success "Answer"
    Volume infused:
    \[150 \text{ mL/hr} \times 3 \text{ hr} = 450 \text{ mL}\]
    
    Volume remaining:
    \[1000 - 450 = 550 \text{ mL}\]
    
    Time remaining:
    \[\frac{550 \text{ mL}}{150 \text{ mL/hr}} = 3.67 \text{ hr}\]
    
    Convert decimal:
    \[0.67 \times 60 = 40 \text{ min}\]
    
    **3 hours and 40 minutes remaining**

## Section 7 — Drip Rates

!!! example "Problem 20"
    Order: 1000 mL over 8 hours
    Tubing: 20 gtt/mL
    What is the drip rate in gtt/min?

??? success "Answer"
    Convert time:
    \[8 \times 60 = 480 \text{ min}\]
    
    Calculate:
    \[\frac{1000 \text{ mL}}{480 \text{ min}} \times 20 \text{ gtt/mL} = 41.7 \text{ gtt/min}\]
    
    Round to: **42 gtt/min**

!!! example "Problem 21"
    Rate: 60 mL/hr
    Tubing: 60 gtt/mL (microdrip)
    What is the drip rate?

??? success "Answer"
    Using microdrip shortcut:
    
    **60 gtt/min**

## Section 8 — IV Medications

!!! example "Problem 22"
    Order: vancomycin 1000 mg in 250 mL over 90 minutes
    What is the flow rate in mL/hr?

??? success "Answer"
    Convert time:
    \[90 \cancel{\text{ min}} \times \frac{1 \text{ hr}}{60 \cancel{\text{ min}}} = 1.5 \text{ hr}\]
    
    Rate:
    \[\frac{250 \text{ mL}}{1.5 \text{ hr}} = 166.7 \text{ mL/hr}\]
    
    Round to: **167 mL/hr**

!!! example "Problem 23"
    Order: heparin 1000 units/hr IV
    Stock: 25,000 units in 500 mL 0.9% NaCl
    What is the flow rate in mL/hr?

??? success "Answer"
    Concentration:
    \[\frac{25{,}000 \text{ units}}{500 \text{ mL}} = 50 \text{ units/mL}\]
    
    Flow rate:
    \[\frac{1000 \text{ units/hr}}{50 \text{ units/mL}} = 20 \text{ mL/hr}\]

!!! example "Problem 24"
    Order: dopamine 5 mcg/kg/min IV
    Patient weight: 60 kg
    Stock: dopamine 400 mg in 250 mL D5W
    What is the flow rate in mL/hr?

??? success "Answer"
    Dose per minute:
    \[60 \cancel{\text{ kg}} \times \frac{5 \text{ mcg}}{1 \cancel{\text{ kg}} \cdot \text{min}} = 300 \text{ mcg/min}\]
    
    Convert to mg/hr:
    \[300 \cancel{\text{ mcg/min}} \times \frac{1 \cancel{\text{ mg}}}{1000 \cancel{\text{ mcg}}} \times \frac{60 \text{ min}}{1 \text{ hr}} = 18 \text{ mg/hr}\]
    
    Concentration:
    \[\frac{400 \text{ mg}}{250 \text{ mL}} = 1.6 \text{ mg/mL}\]
    
    Flow rate:
    \[\frac{18 \text{ mg/hr}}{1.6 \text{ mg/mL}} = 11.25 \text{ mL/hr}\]
    
    Round to: **11 mL/hr**

!!! example "Problem 25"
    An IV is running at 25 mL/hr.
    Stock: morphine 50 mg in 500 mL 0.9% NaCl
    What dose is the patient receiving per hour?

??? success "Answer"
    Concentration:
    \[\frac{50 \text{ mg}}{500 \text{ mL}} = 0.1 \text{ mg/mL}\]
    
    Dose rate:
    \[25 \text{ mL/hr} \times 0.1 \text{ mg/mL} = 2.5 \text{ mg/hr}\]
