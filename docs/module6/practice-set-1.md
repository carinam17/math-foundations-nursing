## Section 1 — Unit Conversions

!!! example "Problem 1"
    Convert 2.5 g to mg.

??? success "Answer"
    $$\dfrac{2.5 \cancel{\text{ g}}}{1} \times \frac{1000 \text{ mg}}{1 \cancel{\text{ g}}} = 2500 \text{ mg}$$

!!! example "Problem 2"
    Convert 750 mcg to mg.

??? success "Answer"
    $$\dfrac{750 \cancel{\text{ mcg}}}{} \times \frac{1 \text{ mg}}{1000 \cancel{\text{ mcg}}} = 0.75 \text{ mg}$$

!!! example "Problem 3"
    A patient weighs 143 lb. Convert to kg.

??? success "Answer"
    $$\dfrac{143 \cancel{\text{ lb}}}{1} \times \frac{1 \text{ kg}}{2.2 \cancel{\text{ lb}}} = 65 \text{ kg}$$

## Section 4 — Weight-Based Dosing (Adult)

!!! example "Problem 10"
    **Order:** gentamicin 5 mg/kg IV &nbsp;&nbsp;
    **Patient weight:** 80 kg &nbsp;&nbsp;
    **Stock:** 40 mg/mL&nbsp;&nbsp;

    **How many mL?**

??? success "Answer"
    \[\frac{80 \cancel{\text{ kg}}}{1} \times \frac{5 \cancel{\text{ mg}}}{1 \cancel{\text{ kg}}} \times \frac{1 \text{ mL}}{40 \cancel{\text{ mg}}} = 10 \text{ mL}\]

!!! example "Problem 11"
    **Order:** vancomycin 15 mg/kg IV&nbsp;&nbsp;
    **Patient** weight: 176 lb&nbsp;&nbsp;
    **Stock:** 500 mg/10 mL 

    **How many mL?**

??? success "Answer"

Convert weight:

$$\dfrac{176 \cancel{\text{ lb}}}{1}\times \frac{1 \text{ kg}}{2.2 \cancel{\text{ lb}}} = 80 \text{ kg}$$
    
Full chain:

$$\frac{80 \cancel{\text{ kg}}}{1} \times \frac{15 \cancel{\text{ mg}}}{1 \cancel{\text{ kg}}} \times \frac{10 \text{ mL}}{500 \cancel{\text{ mg}}} = 24 \text{ mL}$$


!!! example "Problem 12"
    **Order:** ibuprofen 10 mg/kg orally, max 600 mg &nbsp;&nbsp;
    **Patient** weight: 85 kg &nbsp;&nbsp;
    **Stock:** 200 mg/5 mL

    **How many mL?**

??? success "Answer"

Convert weight:


$$\dfrac{85 \cancel{\text{ kg}}}{1} \times \frac{10 \text{ mg}}{1 \cancel{\text{ kg}}} = 850 \text{ mg}$$
    
850 mg exceeds max of 600 mg. **Use 600 mg** to find volume.
    
Find volume:

$$\frac{600 \cancel{\text{ mg}}}{1} \times \frac{5 \text{ mL}}{200 \cancel{\text{ mg}}} = 15 \text{ mL}$$


!!! example "Problem 13"
    **Order:** morphine 0.1 mg/kg IV PRN &nbsp;&nbsp;
    **Patient** weight: 154 lb &nbsp;&nbsp;
    **Stock:** 10 mg/mL

    **How many mL?**

??? success "Answer"
Convert weight:
$$ \dfrac{154 \cancel{\text{ lb}}}{1} \times \frac{1 \text{ kg}}{2.2 \cancel{\text{ lb}}} = 70 \text{ kg}$$
    
Full chain:

$$\frac{70 \cancel{\text{ kg}}}{1} \times \frac{0.1 \cancel{\text{ mg}}}{1 \cancel{\text{ kg}}} \times \frac{1 \text{ mL}}{10 \cancel{\text{ mg}}} = 0.7 \text{ mL}$$


**Note:** You can break the factors into separate step if needed.

    **Order:** paracetamol 15 mg/kg orally, max 500 mg &nbsp;&nbsp;
    **Patient:** child weighing 40 kg

    **Stock:** 250 mg/5 mL

    **How many mL?**



??? success "Answer"

Calculate dose:
    
$$\dfrac{40 \cancel{\text{ kg}}}{1} \times \frac{15 \text{ mg}}{1 \cancel{\text{ kg}}} = 600 \text{ mg}$$
    
600 mg exceeds max of 500 mg — **use 500 mg**
    
Calculate volume:

$$\dfrac{500 \cancel{\text{ mg}}}{1} \times \frac{5 \text{ mL}}{250 \cancel{\text{ mg}}} = 10 \text{ mL}$$


!!! example "Problem 16"
    **Order:** penicillin V 50 mg/kg/day orally &nbsp;&nbsp;
    divided every 6 hours &nbsp;&nbsp;

    **Patient:** child weighing 20 kg

    **Stock:** 125 mg/5 mL

    **How many mL per dose?**


??? success "Answer"

Total daily dose:


$$\dfrac{20 \cancel{\text{ kg}}}{1}  \times \dfrac{50 \text{ mg}}{1 \cancel{\text{ kg} \cdot  \text{day}} = 1000 \text{ mg/day}$$



$$\dfrac{20 \cancel{\text{ kg} \cdot \text{day} \times \frac{5 \text{ mL}}{125 \cancel{\text{ mg}}} = 10 \text{ mL}$$


Single dose (4 doses/day):

$$1000 \div 4 = 250 \text{ mg per dose}$$
    

Volume:

$$\dfrac{1000 \cancel{\text{ mg}}}{1 \text{ day}} \times \frac{5 \text{ mL}}{125 \cancel{\text{ mg}}} = 10 \text{ mL}$$

## Section 6 — IV Flow Rates

!!! example "Problem 17"
    Order: 1000 mL 0.9% NaCl over 8 hours
    What is the flow rate in mL/hr?

??? success "Answer"
    \[\frac{1000 \text{ mL}}{8 \text{ hr}} = 125 \text{ mL/hr}\]

!!! example "Problem 18"
    Order: 250 mL over 45 minutes
    What is the flow rate in mL/hr?

??? success "Answer"
    Convert time:
    \[45 \cancel{\text{ min}} \times \frac{1 \text{ hr}}{60 \cancel{\text{ min}}} = 0.75 \text{ hr}\]
    
    Rate:
    \[\frac{250 \text{ mL}}{0.75 \text{ hr}} = 333.3 \text{ mL/hr}\]
    
    Round to: **333 mL/hr**

!!! example "Problem 19"
    A 1000 mL bag is running at 150 mL/hr.

    After 3 hours how much fluid remains and how much longer will it run?

??? success "Answer"

Volume infused:

$$\dfrac{150 \text{ mL}}{1 \cancel{\text{hr}}} \times \dfrac{3 \cancel{\text{ hr}}}{1} = 450 \text{ mL}$$
    

Volume remaining:
$$ 1000 \text{ mL} - 450\text{ mL} = 550 \text{ mL} $$


Time remaining:

$$\dfrac{550 \text{ mL}}{1} \times \dfrac {1 \text{ hr}}{{150 \text{ mL}}} = 3.67 \text{ hr}$$
    

Convert partial hour to minutes: 

Total time = 3 hours + 0.67 hours

$$\dfrac{0.67 \text{ hr}}{1} \times \dfrac{1 \text{ hr}}{60 \text{ min}} = 40 \text{ min}$$
    
**3 hours and 40 minutes remaining**

## Section 7 — Drip Rates

!!! example "Problem 20"
    **Order:** 1000 mL over 8 hours
    **Tubing:** 20 gtt/mL
    **What is the drip rate in gtt/min?**

??? success "Answer"
    Convert time:
    \[8 \times 60 = 480 \text{ min}\]
    
    Calculate:
    \[\frac{1000 \text{ mL}}{480 \text{ min}} \times 20 \text{ gtt/mL} = 41.7 \text{ gtt/min}\]
    
    Round to: **42 gtt/min**

## Section 8 — IV Medications

!!! example "Problem 22"
    Order: vancomycin 1000 mg in 250 mL over 90 minutes
    What is the flow rate in mL/hr?

??? success "Answer"
    Convert time:
    \[90 \cancel{\text{ min}} \times \frac{1 \text{ hr}}{60 \cancel{\text{ min}}} = 1.5 \text{ hr}\]
    
    Rate:
    \[\frac{250 \text{ mL}}{1.5 \text{ hr}} = 166.7 \text{ mL/hr}\]
    
    Round to: **167 mL/hr**


!!! example "Problem 23"
    Order: heparin 1000 units/hr IV
    Stock: 25,000 units in 500 mL 0.9% NaCl
    What is the flow rate in mL/hr?

??? success "Answer"
    Concentration:
    \[\frac{25{,}000 \text{ units}}{500 \text{ mL}} = 50 \text{ units/mL}\]
    
    Flow rate:
    \[\frac{1000 \text{ units/hr}}{50 \text{ units/mL}} = 20 \text{ mL/hr}\]

!!! example "Problem 24"
    Order: dopamine 5 mcg/kg/min IV
    Patient weight: 60 kg
    Stock: dopamine 400 mg in 250 mL D5W
    What is the flow rate in mL/hr?


??? success "Answer"
Dose per minute:

$$ \dfrac{60 \cancel{\text{ kg}}}{1} \times \frac{5 \text{ mcg}}{1 \cancel{\text{ kg}} \cdot \text{min}} = 300 \text{ mcg/min}$$
    

Convert to mg/hr:

$$\dfrac{300 \cancel{\text{ mcg}}{1 \text{min}} \times \frac{1 \cancel{\text{ mg}}}{1000 \cancel{\text{ mcg}}} \times \frac{60 \text{ min}}{1 \text{ hr}} = 18 \text{ mg/hr}$$
    

Concentration:
    \[\frac{400 \text{ mg}}{250 \text{ mL}} = 1.6 \text{ mg/mL}\]
    
    Flow rate:
    \[\frac{18 \text{ mg/hr}}{1.6 \text{ mg/mL}} = 11.25 \text{ mL/hr}\]
    
    Round to: **11 mL/hr**

!!! example "Problem 25"

    An IV is running at 25 mL/hr.

    **Stock:** morphine 50 mg in 500 mL 0.9% NaCl

    **What dose is the patient receiving per hour?**

??? success "Answer"
Concentration:

$$\frac{50 \text{ mg}}{500 \text{ mL}} = 0.1 \text{ mg/mL}$$
    
Dose rate:

$$\dfrac{0.1 \text{ mg}}{1 \text{mL}} \times  \dfrac{25 \text{ mL}}{1 \text{hr}} \ = 2.5 \text{ mg/hr}$$
